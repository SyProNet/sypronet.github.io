Abstract

Infrared–visible image fusion aims to generate a single image that preserves complementary information from both modalities, serving as a fundamental task in multimodal perception and scene understanding. Although prior-guided fusion methods have achieved notable progress in recent years, existing approaches still face challenges in effectively modeling modality-specific characteristics and designing dynamic prior guidance mechanisms. To address these limitations, we propose SyProNet, a novel fusion framework that integrates triple prior guidance with synergistic prompts. First, we introduce a dual-teacher distillation scheme to generate modality-specific prompts, specifically the thermal saliency prior and the spatial quality distribution prior, injecting this knowledge in a more generalizable manner. Then, we design a coarse-to-fine interactive fusion strategy: at the coarse level, cross-attention facilitates fundamental information exchange; at the fine level, perceptual adaptation priors are incorporated into a Ternary-Refinement Branch (TRB) that jointly models modal commonalities, modality-specific traits, and cross-modal priors. These features are further refined through a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) network with iterative feedback, and finally reconstructed into a high-quality fused image. Extensive experiments demonstrate that SyProNet effectively leverages integrated priors to dynamically reweight cross-modal features, consistently delivering superior fusion quality across diverse scenarios and outperforming state-of-the-art methods on multiple benchmarks.
